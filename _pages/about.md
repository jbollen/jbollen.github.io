I am a Professor of Simulation of Complex Adaptive Systems at the [University of Amsterdam](https://ivi.uva.nl/) 🇳🇱 where I am a member of the [Computational Science Lab](https://ivi.uva.nl/research/computational-science-lab-csl.html) of the [Informatics Institute](https://ivi.uva.nl/), and Professor of [Informatics](https://luddy.iu.edu/academics/units/informatics.html) and [Cognitive Science](https://cogs.indiana.edu/index.html) at [Indiana University Bloomington](https://luddy.iu.edu/) 🇺🇸.

I served as [Informatics](https://luddy.iu.edu/academics/units/informatics.html) department chair at [Indiana University Bloomington](https://luddy.iu.edu/) where I am presently the director of the Center for Social and Biomedical Complexity. I am a member of the board of directors of the center for [Urban Mental Health](https://ias.uva.nl/research-themes/health-complexity/urban-mental-health/urban-mental-health.html), director of the SOCIAL lab at Indiana University, and a [Synergy Program for Analysing Resilience and Critical transitionS](https://sparcs-center.org/) fellow at the [Wageningen University and Research](https://www.wur.nl/). I was formerly a staff scientist at the [Los Alamos National Laboratory](https://www.lanl.gov/) in New Mexico and an Assistant Professor at the [Department of Computer Science of Old Dominion University](https://www.odu.edu/academics/programs/linked/computer-science) in Virginia. I have a PhD (2001) in Experimental Psychology from the [Vrije Universiteit Brussel](https://www.vub.be/nl)🇧🇪.

I published more than 100 articles on computational science, computational psychology, AI, network science, complex systems, social media analytics, and informetrics, receiving nearly 25,000 citations (h-index=50, i10=100). My research has been funded by the NIH, NSF, DARPA, IARPA, EDA, NASA, and Andrew W. Mellon Foundation. My recent work is focused on aunraveling the complex dynamics of individual and collective mental health, with respect to the affective, cognitive, and behavioral effects of social media on well-being.
