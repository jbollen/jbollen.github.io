Johan Bollen is a computational scientist exploring the dynamics of human and social behavior in the context of techno-social systems. He holds professorships at the [University of Amsterdam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Amsterdam) 🇳🇱, where he focuses on the simulation of complex adaptive systems, and at [Indiana University Bloomington](https://www.indiana.edu/) 🇺🇸, where he bridges informatics and cognitive science. At Indiana University, he previously served as Chair of the Informatics department of the [Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering](https://luddy.iu.edu/index.html), now directs the Center for Social and Biomedical Complexity (CSBC), and leads the SOCIAL Lab. He serves on the board of directors of the [Center for Urban Mental Health](https://www.centreforurbanmentalhealth.com/) at the University of Amsterdam [Institute for Advanced studies](https://ias.uva.nl/). He is a Synergy Program for Analysing Resilience and Critical transitionS (SPARCS) fellow at [Wageningen University and Research](https://www.wur.nl/en.htm).

Bollen’s career spans academic institutions, national laboratories, and the private sector. He was a staff scientist at the [Los Alamos National Laboratory](https://www.lanl.gov/) in New Mexico and an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at [Old Dominion University](https://www.odu.edu/) in Virginia. He earned his PhD in Psychology from the [Vrije Universiteit Brussel](https://www.vub.be/en)🇧🇪 in 2001. He is also an entrepreneur, founding Guidewave Consulting to translate social media sentiment into financial signals, co-founding [Wordsentry](https://finovate.com/videos/finovatefall-2013-wordsentry/) which he served as lead theorist, and holding two patents in computational modeling and behavioral finance.

With over 100 publications and nearly 25,000 citations (h‑index = 50), Bollen has contributed widely to computational science, AI, network analysis, social media analytics, informetrics, and the dynamics of collective behavior. His research has been supported by [National Institutes of Health (NIH)](https://www.nih.gov/), [National Science Foundation (NSF)](https://www.nsf.gov/), [Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA)](https://www.darpa.mil/), [Intelligence Advanced Research Projects Activity (IARPA)](https://www.iarpa.gov/), [U.S. Department of Defense (EDA)](https://www.eda.mil/), [NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/), and the [Andrew W. Mellon Foundation](https://mellon.org/). His work emphasizes the societal and commercial impact of digital technologies, particularly how social media shapes mental health, decision‑making, and collective dynamics. By combining computational modeling with applied research, Bollen’s work provides insights that inform public policy, guide industry stakeholders, and inspire evidence‑based interventions to improve well‑being and foster innovation across sectors.
