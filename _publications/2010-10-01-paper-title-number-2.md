excerpt: 'The surge of post-truth political argumentation suggests that we are living in a special historical period when it comes to the balance between emotion and reasoning. We show that the use of words associated with rationality, such as “determine” and “conclusion,” rose systematically after 1850, while words related to human experience such as “feel” and “believe” declined. This pattern reversed over the past decades, paralleled by a shift from a collectivistic to an individualistic focus...'
